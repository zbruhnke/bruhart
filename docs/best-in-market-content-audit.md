## Executive Conclusion

Bru-Hart can credibly threaten Master Halco and the other major distributors, but not by publishing hundreds of average pages. The current 205-URL footprint gives Bru-Hart enough surface area. The next move is to make priority pages more useful than competitor pages.

Master Halco's strongest pages win because they reduce uncertainty. They give buyers product subnavigation, applications, specs, brochures, warranties, drawings, and animal/use-case guidance. TYMETAL, Delta, Ameristar, and HySecurity win high-security pages by showing ratings, clear openings, downloads, CAD/specs, applications, product notes, and spec-team access. Ametco wins component/gate pages by showing project photos, CAD drawings, infill options, and engineering support. Local agricultural competitors win because they speak plainly about Florida conditions, animal safety, post depth, top boards, no-climb wire, and real installation concerns.

Bru-Hart can be better by combining those strengths with something the big players do not show enough: Dana-level field judgment, sourcing counsel, what-not-to-buy guidance, special-order realism, manufacturer fit, quote checklists, and real proof.

## The Simple Rule

A Bru-Hart page should make the buyer smarter before they call.

If a page only says "we sell chain link" or "we carry gate hardware," it will not beat the market. If it explains what to choose, what to avoid, what to measure, what photos to send, which manufacturer paths are real, and what goes wrong when buyers cheap out, it becomes a serious threat.

## What Best-In-Market Looks Like For Bru-Hart

### 1. Every Priority Page Has A "Send Us This" Box

This is the simplest conversion advantage.

Examples:

| Page Type | Ask The Buyer To Send |
| --- | --- |
| Chain link | Total footage, height, fabric gauge, mesh, coating, terminal count, gates, privacy needs, photos. |
| Pipe/tube | OD, wall/gauge, length, finish, quantity, use case, import/domestic requirement, delivery/pickup need. |
| Gate operator | Gate type, width, weight, cycle count, power, safety devices, access control, photos, current operator. |
| Crash-rated gate | Required rating, clear opening, backspace, infill, power, operator, controls, site drawings, agency/spec requirements. |
| Farm fence | Animal type, acreage/footage, terrain, corners, gate widths, equipment access, post preference, photos. |
| Replacement hardware | Photos, dimensions, brand/model numbers, gate material, frame size, failure mode, urgency. |

### 2. Every Priority Page Says "Choose This When / Avoid This When"

This is how Bru-Hart turns expertise into content.

Examples:

| Product | Choose This When | Avoid This When |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Galvanized chain link | Cost, durability, and function matter more than appearance. | The site needs high visual screening, anti-cut performance, or premium aesthetics. |
| No-climb horse fence | Hoof safety and containment matter. | The buyer wants only a cheap perimeter fence for cattle pasture. |
| Cantilever gate | Ground track is a problem, weather/debris is a concern, or the opening needs smoother operation. | There is not enough backspace or the gate package cannot support the weight. |
| Crash-rated gate | Vehicle mitigation is required at an access point. | The site only needs traffic control and no rating documentation is required. |
| Imported product | It is the best-performing or best-available fit for the job. | Domestic sourcing is contractually required or replacement support is uncertain. |
