# Bru-Hart URL Quality Benchmark

Date: April 26, 2026

For the deeper best-in-market scan with exact URLs and page-by-page takeaways, see `docs/best-in-market-content-audit.md`.

Scope: Per-URL quality review of major national and local competitors referenced in the Bru-Hart SEO plan.

## Executive Read

Bru-Hart is now competitive by raw sitemap count, but raw URL count is not enough. Master Halco's best pages are stronger than a typical generated landing page because they include product subnavigation, downloadable specs, brochures, warranties, drawings, application details, and category-specific education. Binford's pages are thinner, but their best pages use strong sourcing language around import/domestic product, cut-to-order work, Schedule 80 sourcing, special orders, and "no matter the complexity." Fencing Supply Group's URL strength is mostly location and brand scale. Stephens Pipe & Steel has broad product/category coverage, location coverage, resources, drawings, tools, and third-party technical-support proof, but its official pages are sparse and dated.

The opportunity is not to publish more pages than everyone else. The opportunity is to make Bru-Hart's important pages feel more useful, specific, and decision-ready than competitor URLs.

## What A Competition-Grade Bru-Hart URL Needs

Every priority URL should be scored against this checklist before being treated as a page Bru-Hart expects to compete with.

| Module | Required Standard | Why It Beats Competitors |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Intent-specific H1 and title | Exact product, use case, local market, or expert problem in the H1/title. | Prevents broad pages from trying to rank for precise SERPs. |
| Buyer problem | State the actual reason the buyer is searching, not just the category name. | Moves Bru-Hart beyond commodity catalog copy. |
| Product/application fit | Explain when the product is right, wrong, overkill, or underpowered. | Turns expertise into visible content. |
| Spec or RFQ table | Measurements, material, coating, rating, gauge, size, operator, lead time, or photos needed. | Creates quote quality and spec depth competitors often lack. |
| Decision rules | "Choose X when..." and "avoid Y when..." sections. | Makes the page useful even before a sales call. |
| Product availability truth | Stocked, sourced, special-order, quote-only, manufacturer-dependent, or verify-before-bid. | Prevents overpromising and builds trust. |
| Manufacturer context | Which manufacturers/brands may matter and why, without unauthorized relationship claims. | Uses manufacturer reputation without pretending every product is available. |
| Proof asset | Photo, testimonial, owner note, case study, project example, or placeholder asking for proof only if real. | Separates real authority from generated copy. |
| Downloads or diagrams | Spec sheet, sell sheet, checklist, drawing, PDF, or diagram where possible. | Master Halco is strong here; Bru-Hart needs to match or beat it on priority pages. |
| Internal links | Links to product, resource, manufacturer, local, industry, proof, and contact pages. | Turns the site into a cluster rather than isolated landing pages. |
| CTA matched to intent | Request quote, ask an expert, send photos/specs, check availability, or call for a hard sourcing problem. | Better conversion than generic "contact us." |
| Last-reviewed discipline | Page should have an internal review date/owner once content moves into CMS. | Important for specs, product availability, standards, and manufacturer language. |

| Tier | Use | Minimum Quality |

| Tier 1: Money / reputation pages | Crash-rated gates, expert sourcing, BH Track, local Branford ag, top product categories, data center/utility, manufacturer pages with demand. | Custom copy, RFQ checklist, proof module, photos, related links, spec/download plan, reviewed claims. |

5. Add downloadable PDFs for BH Track, CNC trucks, complex gate RFQ, farm fence material list, and crash-rating terminology.
6. Build product-specific comparison modules that competitors do not have: cheap vs correct, stocked vs special-order, imported vs domestic, replacement vs redesign, field substitution risk.

8. Build local/ag pages around Florida-specific soil, livestock, heat, humidity, storm exposure, gate widths, posts, braces, and repair materials.
9. Add "send us this" CTAs: photos, measurements, spec, old model number, opening width, animal type, gate weight, operator type, deadline.
10. Add internal "page owner / reviewed date / claim status" governance before this becomes a 300-page site.

## Per-URL Editorial Gate

No Tier 1 or Tier 2 URL should be considered finished until it answers these questions:

1. What decision is the buyer trying to make?
2. What can go wrong if they buy the cheapest or most obvious option?
3. What details does Bru-Hart need to quote or advise correctly?
4. What product, manufacturer, material, or spec options should be considered?
5. What should the buyer avoid?
6. What proof does Bru-Hart have or need to collect?
7. What internal page should the user visit next?
8. What exact action should the user take now?

## Implementation Recommendation

The current generic `SeoLandingPage` component is useful for fast expansion, but it needs optional enrichment modules before Bru-Hart can outperform competitors per URL.

Add optional fields to the content model:

| Field | Purpose |
| --- | --- |
| `decisionMatrix` | Product/use-case comparison tables. |
| `rfqChecklist` | Structured quote inputs. |
| `downloads` | PDFs, sell sheets, drawings, checklists. |
| `proofAssets` | Testimonials, photos, case-study links, owner notes. |
| `availabilityNotes` | Stocked/sourced/special-order/quote-only truth. |
| `avoidWhen` | Expert guidance on when a product is wrong. |
| `reviewStatus` | Draft, reviewed, claim-checked, publish-ready. |

This lets Bru-Hart keep the 205-page footprint while upgrading the pages that should actually rank and convert.
