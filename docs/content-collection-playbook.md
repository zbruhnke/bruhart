## The Standard

Bru-Hart should not try to beat Master Halco by writing longer generic pages. Master Halco's strongest pages already have category navigation, product subpages, brochures, specifications, warranties, drawings, color systems, applications, and animal/use-case pages. Bru-Hart beats that by adding what large distributor pages usually do not have: visible expert judgment, RFQ guidance, sourcing honesty, hard-problem examples, and proof from real fence/gate experience.

A competition-grade Bru-Hart page should answer:

1. What decision is the buyer trying to make?
2. What can go wrong if they buy the cheapest or most obvious product?
3. What information does Bru-Hart need to quote or advise correctly?
4. Which products, manufacturers, or materials should be considered?
5. Which options should be avoided for this use case?
6. What proof exists: photo, quote, case study, owner note, spec sheet, or manufacturer document?
7. What should the buyer send or do next?

## What I Need From You

### 1. Product Truth

I need a working product and sourcing matrix. It does not need to be polished.

| Field | Example |
| --- | --- |
| Category | Chain link fabric, gate operators, farm gates, BH Track, Locinox locks |
| Stock status | Stocked, sourced, special-order, quote-only, not currently supported |

| Lead-time notes | Normal, volatile, special-order, confirm before bid |

Minimum useful version: 50 rows covering the highest-value categories.

### 2. Hard-Problem Stories

This is the moat. I need raw stories where Bru-Hart, Dana, or the team knew something the average supplier would miss.

For each story, send:

| Prompt | What To Capture |
| --- | --- |
| What was the buyer trying to do? | Facility, contractor, landowner, fabricator, project type |
| What made it hard? | Missing spec, unusual size, wrong product assumption, lead time, compatibility, rating, access-control issue |
| What did the average supplier likely miss? | The mistake or blind spot |
| What did Bru-Hart recommend? | Product, manufacturer, substitution, sourcing path, fabrication, or warning |
| What happened? | Faster quote, correct fit, avoided callback, found product, better package |
| Can we use names/photos? | Public, anonymized, internal-only, needs approval |

Minimum useful version: 15 stories. Ideal: 50.

### 3. Owner Voice And Opinions

| Question | Why It Matters |

### 5. Manufacturer And Claim Boundaries

For every manufacturer page or brand reference, I need:

| Field | Required Answer |
| --- | --- |
| Can Bru-Hart publicly say it carries/supports this brand? | Yes/no/needs approval |

| Stocked or sourced? | Stocked/sourced/special-order/quote-only |
| Any trademark/dealer language to avoid? | Exact forbidden wording |
| Any approved documents? | URLs/PDFs/specs |

This prevents fake authority and protects the business.

## Interview Agenda

The fastest path is a recorded interview with the owner or most knowledgeable operator.

### Session 1: The Hard Problems

1. What are the top 10 calls Bru-Hart wants that Master Halco/Binford/Stephens might mishandle?
2. What products or parts are hard to find but worth knowing how to source?
3. What does a bad gate package look like?
4. What product substitutions are acceptable, and which are dangerous?
5. Where do contractors lose money because they bought the wrong thing?
6. What does Dana know that needs to be public?

### Session 2: Product Families

1. Chain link: gauges, coatings, posts, fittings, gates, slats.
2. Pipe/tube: wall thickness, import/domestic, special order, cut length, gate frames.
3. Gates/operators: swing, slide, cantilever, vertical lift, access control, safety.
4. Agricultural: cattle, horse, goat/sheep, field fence, fixed knot, barbed wire, farm gates.
5. Hardware: hinges, latches, locks, rollers, trucks, guides, closers.
6. Specialty/security: crash-rated gates, barriers, anti-climb, bollards, critical sites.

### Session 3: Proof And Reputation

1. Who can give a testimonial quickly?
2. Which projects can become public case studies?
3. Which photos can be used immediately?
4. Which manufacturers would approve quotes, documents, or logos?
5. Which claims should stay private or be softened?

If you want to move fast, send this first:

1. A rough manufacturer/product list.
2. A stocked vs sourced vs special-order list, even if imperfect.
3. 20 photos of products, yard, gates, hardware, BH Track, CNC trucks, agricultural materials.
4. 10 hard-problem stories in rough bullet form.
5. 5 testimonials or people likely to give them.
6. Any existing PDFs, sell sheets, drawings, spec sheets, or manufacturer docs.
7. A recording/transcript with Dana answering the interview agenda.

With that, I can start upgrading Tier 1 pages immediately.

## Quality Gate Before Publishing A Tier 1 Page

Do not treat a Tier 1 page as finished unless it has:

1. One specific buyer problem.
2. One RFQ checklist.
3. One decision matrix or choose/avoid section.
4. One proof asset or explicitly marked proof-needed slot.
5. One availability/sourcing truth section.
6. One manufacturer/spec/document reference if applicable.
7. Five or more internal links to supporting pages.
8. A CTA that asks for the right next input, not just "contact us."
