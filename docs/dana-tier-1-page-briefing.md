## The Core Point

Bru-Hart should not try to beat Master Halco, Binford, Stephens, Fence Supply Group, and local North Florida fence competitors by simply publishing more words.

Bru-Hart should beat them by making the expertise visible.

The strongest version of the site should make a buyer feel:

| Buyer Thought | What The Page Must Show |
| --- | --- |
| "These people know what can go wrong." | Choose/avoid guidance, failure points, field notes, and RFQ checklists. |
| "They can source things other suppliers may not handle well." | Manufacturer options, special-order paths, import/domestic judgment, and hard-problem stories. |
| "They are not just selling junk or chasing the lowest price." | Product-fit reasoning, proof, respected manufacturers, and honest availability notes. |
| "This page helped me know what to send before I call." | Measurements, photos, drawings, site conditions, spec language, and quote inputs. |
| "I should call Bru-Hart before I make a mistake." | Owner expertise, product decision support, and examples of problems avoided. |

## Why We Are Asking For So Much

Large distributors already have breadth. Master Halco's chain link pages show category navigation, downloads, brochures, specifications, mesh/gauge information, warranties, drawings, coating education, slats, and applications. Their agricultural section has animal/use-case structure and downloadable catalog/brochure support. TYMETAL's crash-rated gate pages include product notes, clear-opening limits, product summary, photos, downloads, CAD/PDF resources, and feature/benefit language. Binford's pipe/tube page quickly communicates import/domestic availability, cut-to-order support, Schedule 80 sourcing, and special-order help.

Bru-Hart does not need to copy those pages exactly. Bru-Hart needs to match the buyer usefulness and then add the thing they usually do not have: Dana-level field judgment.

## The Line To Walk

If major distributors, contractors, or manufacturers call Dana when a job gets difficult, that is powerful internal evidence of expertise. We should use the positioning carefully.

| Do Say | Do Not Say Without Approval |
| --- | --- |
| "Bru-Hart brings decades of fence and gate judgment to hard sourcing and product-fit questions." | "A named competitor relies on Bru-Hart." |
| "Large distributors and contractors often need experienced specialists when specs, sourcing, or field problems get difficult." | "Competitor X privately depends on Dana to fix its mistakes." |
| "Bru-Hart is built for buyers who need the right answer, not just the fastest price sheet." | "Bru-Hart is officially endorsed by a competitor or manufacturer" unless documented. |
| "Some jobs need expert counsel before pricing." | Any private story naming a company, customer, or project without permission. |

This still lets Bru-Hart show the expertise cleanly. We can use anonymized stories, owner notes, and product decision guidance without creating legal, relationship, or reputation risk.

## What A Great Tier 1 Page Contains

Every Tier 1 page should eventually include:

| Module | Why It Helps |
| --- | --- |
| Specific buyer problem | Searchers and sales prospects immediately know the page is for them. |
| Choose / avoid matrix | Shows expertise competitors cannot easily copy. |
| RFQ checklist | Turns the page into a better sales intake tool. |
| Downloads / spec sheets | Matches competitor utility and gives buyers something to save/send. |
| Real photos | Proves Bru-Hart touches real products and jobs. |
| Availability notes | Sets expectations on stocked, sourced, special-order, quote-only, import/domestic. |
| Proof assets | Testimonials, case studies, owner notes, manufacturer docs, or approved examples. |
| Internal links | Helps Google and buyers understand how products, industries, resources, and proof connect. |
| Claim boundaries | Prevents unsafe language like "crash tested" unless exact documentation supports it. |
